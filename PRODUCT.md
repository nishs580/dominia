# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

android

## Users

People who want a reason to walk daily. They are drawn in by the strategy and territory mechanics, but they also want to stay fit, and they are looking for a community of like-minded people who walk. Bengaluru first, English speaking. They play in short sessions on foot and between walks — outdoors in bright light on the map, indoors when checking their alliance, streaks, and standing. The product addresses each of them as a Commander: intelligent, disciplined, capable of long-horizon decisions.

## Product Purpose

Dominia is a city-scale strategy game played on foot. Every territory is a real OpenStreetMap street, park, or plaza. Players claim territories by walking, hold them by returning, and defend them with alliances of up to 20 members. Physical activity is the only resource — distance walked determines what you can claim and contest; nothing can be bought or boosted. Success is a player who keeps a daily walking streak alive because their territory, their alliance, and their standing depend on it.

Tagline: Walk. Claim. Conquer. Defend.

## Positioning

The real world is the game board, and fitness alone decides outcomes — not geography, spend, or luck. Every screen reinforces that the walk is the only currency.

## Brand Personality

A commander's field note: precise, restrained, strategic. Three words — disciplined, permanent, earned. The interface honours effort through precision, never praise; there are no "amazing!"s, no emoji, no exclamation marks. British English throughout. Copy must never imply travelling to a territory — the walk counts from anywhere.

## Anti-references

From the brand brief, Dominia is explicitly NOT:

- A fitness tracker — movement is a mechanic, not the product.
- A casual gamified-exercise app — mechanics are strategic and permanent, no lives to refill.
- A conventional mobile game — no glossy CG renders, character art, loot boxes, or neon.
- A cute, friendly wellness brand — no soft pastels, rounded corners, smiley illustrations, or encouraging-friend voice.
- A military product — strategy vocabulary yes; weapons, armed-forces imagery, or glorified violence, never.

## Design Principles

The five brand pillars, used as tiebreakers when a decision is unclear:

1. Physical activity is the primary resource — visual treatments must respect the effort behind every number.
2. The real world is the game board — the map is the product, not a backdrop; treat it as sacred.
3. Every session has strategic purpose — every element earns its place; no decorative clutter.
4. Consistency is the only currency we celebrate — honour showing up through precision, not praise.
5. Fitness decides outcomes, not geography or spend — never suggest money, location, or demographics matter.
